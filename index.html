About Me

I am a 5th-year BS-MS student in Physics at IISER Bhopal, India. My academic interests include theoretical high-energy physics, quantum field theory, quantum gravity, and cosmology. I come from Bihar, a state in eastern India known for its rich history and deep philosophical traditions.

When I’m not working on a physics problem, I enjoy reading philosophical books, noticing things around me, and playing basketball and table tennis. I’m also fascinated by how ideas evolve—whether in science, society, or individual minds.

Research

  • Pathology in quantum field theories: My broader research interests center around the formulation of quantum field theories, with a particular emphasis on self-interacting vector field theories and their massless limit — the subject of my master's thesis. In addition to this, I have been exploring aspects of general relativity and theoretical frameworks that aim to unify gravity with quantum mechanics.

    Master's Thesis: Self-intracting massive vector fields and its massless limit

    • Preprint:arXiv:2505.22119

  • Ongoing Study: I am also deeply interested in quantum cosmology. Currently, I am investigating quantum information flow in open systems using the influence functional formalism, aiming to model observer-centric dynamics. This line of inquiry is driven by a fundamental question: What does the universe look like to a local observer?.

    • References: arXiv:2505.22119 and arXiv:2505.22119
