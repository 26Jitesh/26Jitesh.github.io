Previous projects

  • High-Harmonic Generation in Confined Hydrogen Atoms: This project focuses on obtaining accurate results for hydrogen-like atoms under intense external fields. We employ a non-perturbative framework to simulate the generation of high-order harmonics induced by ultrashort, intense pulsed laser fields. Our numerical implementation is based on the Split Operator Method combined with a generalized pseudospectral approach, which effectively handles the Coulomb singularity and semi-infinite domain problems. This method was originally developed by Shih-I Chu et al. (1996). We uses this technique to predict the high-harmonic generation (HHG) spectra of endohedrally confined hydrogen atoms — specifically, hydrogen atoms trapped within C60 cages — and compared these results with the spectra of free (unconfined) hydrogen atoms.
  • Dessins d’Enfants and Galois Orbit Prediction: This was my first experience working with abstract mathematical structures like dessins d’enfants. I began by studying foundational topics including Riemann surfaces, Belyi maps, dessins, and Galois actions. I then explored a dataset of dessins in multiple representations — namely, the matrix form and the cyclic edge list form. My analysis started with Principal Component Analysis (PCA) to estimate the intrinsic (true) dimension of the dataset. Finally, I trained a neural network (a multilayer perceptron) to predict the Galois orbit size of each dessin.
